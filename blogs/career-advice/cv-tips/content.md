# Crafting an Academic CV

Your curriculum vitae (CV) is often the first impression you make on hiring committees.  A well organised and readable document can dramatically improve your chances of securing an interview.
![[Pasted image 20260430020338.png]]

## Keep It Clean

Use a clear structure with distinct sections (education, research experience, publications, teaching, awards).  Avoid unnecessary graphics; recruiters value clarity over flashiness.

## Tailor for Each Role

Highlight experiences relevant to the position you are applying for.  For industry roles emphasise transferable skills such as project management and communication.

## Quantify Achievements

Where possible, quantify your accomplishments (e.g. “Increased survey response rate by 40%”).  Numbers stand out and demonstrate impact.
