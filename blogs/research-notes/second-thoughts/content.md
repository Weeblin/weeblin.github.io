# Looking Back

After submitting my thesis I finally had time to reflect on the research journey.  The process was exhilarating and exhausting in equal measure.  This post is less about technical results and more about the human side of research.

## Challenges

1. **Impostor syndrome:** I often doubted whether my work was significant enough.  Speaking with peers and mentors helped me to regain perspective.
2. **Balancing teaching and research:** Managing my time between lecturing and writing required careful planning.
3. **Perfectionism:** There is always one more theorem to prove or example to include.  Eventually you must draw the line and submit!

## Triumphs

Despite the difficulties, there were many rewarding moments—seeing a proof come together, presenting at a conference, and watching students grasp a concept for the first time.

## Advice

If you are embarking on a similar journey, remember to take breaks, seek feedback early and often, and celebrate the small victories.
